
# Key Insights — EIF Fund Managers Analysis

> Findings from the analysis of 12 EIF-backed financial intermediaries, March 2025.

---

## 1. Spain is the Dominant Hub for EIF-Backed Impact Funding

7 out of 12 fund managers in this dataset are Spain-based or Spain-primary. This makes Spain the strongest entry point for companies seeking EIF-backed capital in Southern Europe — particularly for clean energy and sustainability projects. Companies based in or targeting Spain have the widest selection of intermediaries.

---

## 2. Solar Energy Has the Broadest Funding Coverage

Solar projects can access the most fund managers across the dataset — Suma Capital, Alantra, NIAM, Axon Partners, and OXY Capital all explicitly mention solar as a target. This means solar project developers have multiple competing funding options, which strengthens their negotiating position.

---

## 3. Desalination is Underrepresented but Strategically Mapped

No fund manager explicitly lists desalination as a primary focus. However, Impact Bridge's multiple funds (IB Impact Debt, IB Direct Debt, IB Impact Bebt) all align with SDG 6 (Clean Water) and SDG 14 (Life Below Water) — making them the primary route for desalination project funding. The pitch needs to be framed through SDG alignment, not the technology itself.

---

## 4. Green Hydrogen Requires a Cross-Fund Strategy

No single fund covers hydrogen exclusively. The optimal approach is a multi-fund strategy: Suma Capital (infrastructure + clean energy), Impact Bridge (SDG 7), and Axon Partners (deep tech + water treatment). A company pursuing hydrogen should prepare a single coherent pitch adaptable to each fund's framing.

---

## 5. Impact Funds Require SDG Measurement — Prepare for This

Impact Bridge uses a proprietary self-evaluation tool to assess SDG alignment before investment. Norrsken uses a structured application process. Companies approaching these funds must go beyond having a sustainability story — they need **measurable impact metrics** aligned to specific SDGs. This is a preparation requirement, not a barrier.

---

## 6. Nordic Funds Offer the Largest Single EIF Commitments

NIAM (Sweden) carries the largest EIF commitment in the dataset at €51.7M, focused on energy transition and infrastructure. Nordic funds in this dataset operate at a larger ticket size than Iberian funds — better suited for infrastructure-scale projects than early-stage companies.

---

## 7. The Portal Has Significant Untapped Coverage

This analysis covers 12 fund managers. The EU portal lists intermediaries across all 27 EU member states plus EEA countries — representing hundreds of additional opportunities. This dataset is a starting point, not a complete picture. The decision framework in `docs/decision_framework.md` is designed to be reapplied to any filtered subset from the portal.
