# Gap Analysis — EIF Fund Manager Coverage

## Purpose

This analysis identifies funding gaps — areas where company projects have limited or no coverage among the 12 EIF-backed intermediaries analyzed. Understanding gaps helps prioritize where to expand research and where alternative funding strategies are needed.

---

## Coverage Summary

| Project Type | Funds Available | Coverage Level | Gap Severity |
|---|---|---|---|
| Solar Energy | 9 of 12 | Strong | Low |
| Green Hydrogen | 4 of 12 | Moderate | Medium |
| Desalination / Water | 6 of 12 | Moderate | Medium-High |

---

## Gap 1 — Desalination: No Explicit Fund Coverage

**Finding:** No fund manager in this dataset explicitly lists desalination as a primary investment theme. Coverage exists only through indirect SDG alignment (SDG 6 — Clean Water, SDG 14 — Life Below Water).

**Affected funds:** Impact Bridge (IB Impact Debt, IB Direct Debt) — aligned via SDG framing only.

**Risk:** Pitching desalination to these funds requires reframing the project as a water/sanitation/sustainability initiative rather than an infrastructure or technology play. Funds may lack domain expertise to evaluate desalination-specific proposals.

**Recommendation:**
- Frame desalination projects under SDG 6 and SDG 14 when approaching impact funds
- Expand research to EU portal intermediaries with explicit water infrastructure mandates
- Consider EIB direct lending for large-scale desalination (EUR 50M+ tier)

---

## Gap 2 — Green Hydrogen: No Single-Fund Solution

**Finding:** No individual fund covers hydrogen as a standalone theme. Coverage comes from funds that target broader clean energy or decarbonization mandates.

**Affected funds:**
- Suma Capital — via clean energy / decarbonization
- Impact Bridge — via SDG 7 (Renewable Energy)
- Axon Partners — via deep tech / energy transition

**Risk:** Each fund sees hydrogen through a different lens (infrastructure vs. impact vs. tech). A single pitch will not work across all three. The company must prepare tailored narratives per fund type.

**Recommendation:**
- Prepare three pitch variants: infrastructure (Suma), impact/SDG (Impact Bridge), tech/innovation (Axon)
- Explore hydrogen-specific EU funding programs outside the intermediary portal (e.g., Clean Hydrogen Joint Undertaking)
- Monitor for new EIF mandates — hydrogen is a growing EU priority

---

## Gap 3 — Early-Stage / Startup Funding Limited

**Finding:** Only 2 of 12 funds target early-stage companies:
- Norrsken Impact Accelerator (VC / Accelerator)
- Impact Bridge AgriFood Fund (VC)

The majority of funds (PE, Debt, Infrastructure) require established companies with revenue and track records.

**Risk:** If the company or its project subsidiaries are early-stage, most funds in this dataset are not accessible. The accelerator route (Norrsken) has competitive intake and a deadline-driven application process.

**Recommendation:**
- Apply to Norrsken 2026 cohort before deadline
- Expand search to EU portal VC-specific intermediaries
- Consider national startup funding programs as a bridge to EIF-backed VC

---

## Gap 4 — Geographic Concentration in Iberia

**Finding:** 9 of 12 fund managers are Spain-based or include Spain in their scope. Only 2 cover the Nordics, 1 covers Portugal exclusively.

**Implication:** Companies targeting markets outside Iberia (e.g., Germany, France, Eastern Europe) have no coverage in this dataset.

**Recommendation:**
- This dataset is a starting point — use the EU portal decision framework to filter for other countries
- Nordic coverage is available via NIAM and Norrsken but limited to infrastructure and accelerator models
- Portugal has direct coverage via OXY Capital and indirect via Impact Bridge (debt funds)

---

## Gap 5 — Microfinance and Small Loans Not Covered

**Finding:** No fund in this dataset operates in the microfinance or small loan segment (under EUR 500K). All funds operate at EUR 5M+ ticket sizes.

**Recommendation:**
- For smaller funding needs, filter the EU portal by "Microfinance" type
- National promotional banks often handle smaller ticket sizes
- Consider combining microfinance with grant programs for early-stage project development
